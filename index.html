Why Bootcamp at 54?

top

I first became interested in coding over 10 years ago while recovering from a car accident.

As you can imagine from that picture, it was a long recovery & I had time to teach myself the basics of HTML & CSS and dabble in PHP. I actually contemplated trying to make it as a web developer but I had lost the use of my right hand as a result of the accident and lacked the self confidence to make a go of it. I went back to my job as a tax consultant but still kept one eye on the tech world.

Another accident in 2016 (this time a bike crash in a middle distance triathlon that broke 5 bones) led to a bit more time off work. This time I discovered Python and taught myself the basics from books and online tutorials but, again, did nothing with it seriously.

Then came 2020, Covid and lockdown v1.0. My main interest outside work was triathlon and that scene ground to a halt, I lost motivation to train so I had time on my hands. And when I have time on my hands ... yep, Python marched back in to fill the void. One thing led to another and my mind went back 10+ years to my previous flirtation with web development. I started wondering if I could do anything with it seriously because I had been growing increasingly disillusioned with my current career.

On Twitter I bumped into a Manchester Codes student, discovered their Software Engineer FastTrack Bootcamp and the rest, as they say, is history!

So you could say my decision to attend Bootcamp was prompted by:

  • a near-fatal car accident;
  • a bike crash; and,
  • a global pandemic.

Every cloud...

Some might say that 54 is too old to contemplate such a drastic career shift. I disagree: you're never too old to learn new things, especially things you have developed a passion for.

My family

I live with my amazing wife Kirstie, who was in the car with me in the 2007 accident, and our son James who is an amazingly caring, generous, smart 8 year old with a wicked sense of humour. Kirstie is a professional photographer and senior manager with Tropic skincare.

Hobbies & interests

Until the world shut down earlier this year (and I developed a coding obsession) I was a compulsive triathlete training around 10-12 hours a week focusing mainly on the ultra-distance races known as Ironman.

To date, I have completed 8 Ironman races all around Europe: 3 BC (before crash) and 5 after. My best performance was Ironman UK in 2006 where I finished 15th in my age-group with a time of 10:56.

I had an entry for the 10th edition of Ironman Wales in September this year but it didn't go ahead because of the Covid pandemic. My entry has rolled over to 2021 but, to be honest, I think I might have too much on my plate to train properly for it!

Other triathlon-related stuff

I'm a Certified Ironman Coach, British Triathlon Level 1 Coach and during 2018 I set up Thrive Multisport, a triathlon coaching business. Covid has effectively wiped that out for now.

I also founded South Cheshire Triathletes, a small club based in, funnily enough, South Cheshire. Covid hasn't quite killed that off ... yet!
