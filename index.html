Why Bootcamp at 54?

I first became interested in coding over 10 years ago while recovering from a car accident.

It was a long recovery during which I taught myself the basics of HTML & CSS and dabbled in PHP. I actually contemplated trying to make it as a web developer but I had lost the use of my right hand as a result of the accident and lacked the self confidence to make a go of it. I went back to my job as a tax consultant but still kept one eye on the tech world.

Another accident in 2016 (this time a bike crash in a middle distance triathlon that broke 5 bones) led to a bit more time off work. This time I discovered Python and taught myself the basics from books and online tutorials but, again, did nothing with it seriously.

Then came 2020, Covid and lockdown v1.0. My main interest outside work was triathlon and that scene ground to a halt, I lost motivation to train so I had time on my hands. And when I have time on my hands ... yep, Python marched back in to fill the void. One thing led to another and my mind went back 10+ years to my previous flirtation with web development. I started wondering if I could do anything with it seriously because I had been growing increasingly disillusioned with my current career.

On Twitter I bumped into a Manchester Codes student, discovered their Software Engineer FastTrack Bootcamp and the rest, as they say, is history!

So you could say my decision to attend Bootcamp was prompted by:

  • a near-fatal car accident;
  • a bike crash; and,
  • a global pandemic.

Every cloud...

Some might say that 54 is too old to contemplate such a drastic career shift. I disagree: you're never too old to learn new things, especially things you have developed a passion for.

Hobbies & interests

Until the world shut down earlier this year (and I developed a coding obsession) I was a compulsive triathlete training around 10-12 hours a week on average (OCD? What do you mean?). I first discovered triathlon in 2000 and was immediately hooked. In 2005 I had a go at my first Ironman in Nice on the south coast of France. Again I was hooked.

Ironman is an ultra-distance triathlon covering 140.6 miles in total made up of:

Open water swim:2.4 miles
Bike:112 miles
Run:26.2 miles

After Nice in 2005 I competed at Ironman UK in Dorset in 2006 (recording my best ever time of 10:56 and coming 15th in my age group) and Lanzarote in 2007.

4 months after the Lanzarote race my life was turned upside down by a truck.

After recovering from the accident, getting back my triathlon mojo and getting my bikes converted for one-handed use I took on the inaugural Ironman Wales on 11th September 2011 - 4 years to the day since the accident. My time wasn't exactly electric but crossing that finish line was an emotional moment!

I completed 4 other Ironman races over the next few years.
